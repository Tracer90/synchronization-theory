**Abstract:**

We describe a novel propulsion concept based on coupled toroidal field topology and vacuum phase transition dynamics. The Phase-Toroidal Engine (PTE) operates through three coupled mechanisms: (A) nested toroidal electromagnetic confinement producing a directed stress tensor; (B) scalar dilaton mode pumping that locally modulates the vacuum refractive index K, creating a metric gradient; and (C) controlled cycling through a critical topological phase transition, yielding net impulse during symmetry breaking. The theoretical framework connects to recent results in toroidal spin ice phase transitions (Blume-Capel tricritical point, arXiv:2509.01522), dilaton resonance phenomenology (95.4 GeV, CMS/ATLAS), and the Refractive Vacuum Gravity formalism. No reaction mass is required. Falsifiable predictions include Casimir force anomalies at toroidal resonance distances, anomalous thrust from asymmetric RF cavities, and gamma-ray signatures from vacuum phase transition decay.
